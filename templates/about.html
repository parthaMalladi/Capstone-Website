Why I Created Health Finder

I want to create a website that offers users health analytics based on their own health markers. A machine learning algorithm trained on pre-existing datasets will predict the risk of various ailments based on the user’s inputs. My project’s goal is to improve people’s health outcomes by educating people about their health. The website will ask the user for various markers such as age, weight, blood pressure, cholesterol, and so on.

Health Finder provides predictive insights based on trained data, but it is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider with any questions you may have regarding a medical condition and for treatment advice.

Methodology

I Trained machine learning models using Scikit-Learn to predict the likelihood of stroke, type II diabetes, and heart disease. Utilized datasets from UC Irvine, the National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases (NIDDK), and the World Health Organization (WHO). Evaluated model performance using an 80:20 train-test split and assessed metrics including precision, recall, and accuracy to ensure that the ML models are not simply guessing.
